Drinks

Wine

Bouvet Ladubay 'Blanc de Blancs' Crémant de Loire
Established in 1851 by Etienne Bouvet and Celestine Ladubay in Saumur, a Medieval town in the Loire Valley, Bouvet Ladubay winery was endorsed by Napoleon III
and is the official supplier to the House of Lords!
Their 100% Chardonnay vintage crémant, crafted using the traditional French method akin to Champagne, boasts delightful notes of
pear, apple, and white flowers in a refined mousse.

Noughty 0% Sparkling Chardonnay
This vibrant sparkling organic Chardonnay has been entirely de-alcoholised, so provides all of the enjoyment of your favourite bottle of fizz without the alcohol.
It’s fresh and crisp with notes of green apple and fine bubbles.

Cave Vinicole de Hunawihr Kuhlmann-Platz Riesling
This cooperative was started by a group of small producers in Hunawihr in 1954, but today has member growers throughout the Alsace heartland.
Winemaking here is headed by Nicolas Garde, an Alsace-born winemaker, schooled in Burgundy and New Zealand.
We chose this wine pairing after a number of rounds of tastings - and it works beautifully with cured meats, and the heftier flavours of black pudding in our main course.

Villemarin Picpoul de Pinet
Picpoul vines are almost exclusively grown in Picpoul de Pinet – an appellation next to the Languedoc’s Thau lagoon near Montpellier.
It’s incredibly zesty, with intense lemon and grapefruit flavours, and refreshing minerality. It reminded us of apple when we tasted it - a perfect pairing for pork, and a complementary for the risotto.

M. Chapoutier 'Belleruche' Côtes du Rhône
Please note red wine will be served with dinner only.
This wine was made without the use of oak, so it’s fruit-forward. Look out for notes of ripe black cherry, herbs and a kick of peppery spice.
It pairs well with flavoursome meat dishes, especially charcuterie. In 2022, this wine won an IWC award for the 2020 vintage, and when we chose it we could see why - it is absolutely delicious, and won every tasting round when we were choosing.

Spirits

Sapling Vodka
A vodka we originally discovered at Taste of London festival, Sapling plant a tree for every bottle sold.
Made exclusively from British wheat it is four times distilled, resulting in a very clean, crisp, and naturally sweet taste.
The purity brings out herbaceous and citrusy notes which lends itself beautifully to mixing, or drinking on the rocks!

Tears of My Enemies
Tears of My Enemies is a fennel and angelica liqueur, by the Mother of the Bride, Jennifer. Infused on the 2023 'super blue moon', it has been steeping since August - and if you like absinthe, you will love this!
This spirit is best served neat, either over ice or at room temperature. Savour slowly, in a moment of stillness.

Beer

BrewDog Lost Lager
One of their bestselling brews, Lost Lager was crafted as a fresh twist on the norm – a pilsner made with German saphir hops, with notes of vibrant citrus fruits.
Wonderfully crisp and refreshing, our wedding beer being by Brewdog pays tribute to the fact that we met in Scotland!
